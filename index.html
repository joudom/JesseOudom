LocalEyes

React app allowing users to upload photos & post deals found in their local area

Technologies include HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript, ReactJS, React Bootstrap, Git, GitHub, Node.js, Express.js, Bcrypt, Cors, and Google Maps API. Worked on a team of 3 members with front-end design & back-end queries to a database. Took lead on developing user authentication. Took lead on implementing Googles's Map API.


Users can scroll through posts from locals and view items along with their sales description. By displaying a photo of the product and a static Google map of the vendor, the user will know exactly what they are looking at and where to get it. Registered users will have access to upload deals that they may comes across and contribute to the LocalEyes community. The upload feature allows users to enter details of the sales they find and also provide a photo to help others identify the product at hand.

With the edit and delete buttons, user can modify their posts after they are uploaded. All registered users have their credentials stored in a database where their passwords are hashed for security. Logins are only allowed with valid credentials. Users are prompted with error messages if credentials are incorrect or if they do not exist at all. All posts are also stored in the LocalEyes database, which allow posts to be edited and searched for in the application.


At the end of the day, the goal of this application is to serve as a platform for people to help others. Whether it is by saving money or having a sense of being included in a community, LocalEyes aims to bring people together.

Drink-182

Front-end app allowing users to explore cocktail & meal recipes

Technologies include HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript, jQuery, Git, GitHub, Text-to-Speech Reader. Worked on a team of 3 members with front-end design JavaScript functionality. Implemented a filter feature that calls an API to display interactive options for users to sort by. Created a meal feature that displays random images of meals from an API call to possibly pair cocktails with.

This was a very fun project with the team. I was able to gain confidence and knowledge through the collaboration. We were able to tackle the creation of our cocktail search by pushing the different categories from the API call into an empty array. The response was then rendered on the app for users to select their cocktail category via a combonation accordion / toggle button search.

AO14
